# Market-Ready Brief

## Product Name

Rwanda E-Pharmacy

## Product Category

Digital health, e-pharmacy enablement, pharmacy inventory intelligence, medicine access, insurance-aware health commerce, and medication analytics.

## Core Promise

When a patient receives a prescription, Rwanda E-Pharmacy helps them know where the medicine is available, how much it costs, whether their insurance is supported, and whether they can reserve or receive it safely.

## Market-Ready Positioning

Rwanda E-Pharmacy is not just an online shop. It is a regulated medicine access network for Rwanda that connects:

- Patients.
- Licensed retail pharmacies.
- Hospital pharmacies.
- Wholesalers and distributors.
- Health facilities.
- Insurance companies.
- Delivery operators.
- Government and research institutions.

## Market Problem

Patients can lose hours or days moving from pharmacy to pharmacy looking for prescribed medicine. The problem is worse for patients outside Kigali, chronic disease patients, elderly patients, caregivers, and urgent cases.

Pharmacies also struggle with stock visibility, expiry control, manual inventory, missed demand, and weak demand forecasting. Institutions have limited real-time visibility into medicine use and pharmacy-level stock patterns.

## Market-Ready Solution

The product will provide:

- Medicine search by name, brand, generic, category, and active ingredient.
- Prescription photo upload with pharmacist-verified extraction.
- Verified pharmacy availability and price comparison.
- Insurance-aware results.
- Medicine reservation before travelling.
- Delivery requests where legally and safely permitted.
- Chronic disease refill reminders.
- Pharmacy inventory management.
- Low-stock and expiry alerts.
- National medication analytics dashboards using aggregated data.

## First Winning MVP

The first market-ready MVP should prove one full flow:

1. Patient searches or uploads prescription.
2. System identifies medicines.
3. Patient selects location or allows GPS.
4. System shows verified pharmacies with stock, price, distance, insurance support, and last update time.
5. Patient reserves medicine.
6. Pharmacy confirms stock.
7. Patient collects or requests delivery.
8. Admin dashboard shows stock-out and demand trends.

## Why This Can Win

- It solves a painful and common patient problem.
- It creates direct value for pharmacies.
- It aligns with Rwanda's digital health direction.
- It can use Rwanda FDA licensed pharmacy lists for verification.
- It supports price transparency and medicine access.
- It creates a data layer valuable to insurers and public health institutions.

## Non-Negotiables

- Only verified pharmacies should appear as trusted suppliers.
- Prescription medicines require pharmacist oversight.
- Medicine delivery must follow Rwanda FDA rules.
- Health data must be protected.
- Advertisements must be reviewed and clearly labelled.
- Analytics must be privacy-safe.
