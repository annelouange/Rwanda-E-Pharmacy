# Go-To-Market and Operations

## Launch Strategy

Start with a focused pilot, not a national launch.

Recommended pilot:

- Kigali plus one non-Kigali district.
- 20-50 verified pharmacies.
- 100-300 priority medicines.
- 500-2,000 patient users.
- 2-5 clinics or hospitals.
- 1 insurance partner if feasible.

## Pilot Segments

Patient segments:

- Chronic disease patients.
- Caregivers.
- Hospital discharge patients.
- Provincial patients seeking medicine in Kigali.

Pharmacy segments:

- Independent retail pharmacies.
- Pharmacy chains.
- Hospital pharmacies.
- Pharmacies already comfortable with digital tools.

## Pharmacy Onboarding Process

1. Identify pharmacy.
2. Verify license and responsible professional.
3. Create pharmacy account.
4. Train staff.
5. Import initial inventory.
6. Confirm operating hours and insurance partners.
7. Run test reservation.
8. Activate public listing.

## Patient Acquisition

Channels:

- Hospitals and clinics.
- Pharmacy posters.
- WhatsApp campaigns.
- Community health workers.
- Chronic disease groups.
- Social media.
- Insurance member communications.

## Operations Model

Support center:

- Patient support.
- Pharmacy support.
- Delivery escalation.
- Prescription upload issues.
- Complaint management.

Daily operations:

- Monitor stale stock.
- Monitor failed reservations.
- Monitor delivery problems.
- Review new pharmacy applications.
- Review ads.

Weekly operations:

- Data quality report.
- Pharmacy engagement report.
- Stock-out report.
- Support issue review.

## Sales Motion

Phase 1:

- Free pharmacy onboarding.
- Prove patient demand.

Phase 2:

- Sell pharmacy Pro tools.
- Sell ads to approved health businesses.
- Sell dashboard pilots to institutions.

Phase 3:

- Institutional contracts.
- API integrations.
- National partnerships.

## Brand Promise

"Find the medicine before you travel."

Supporting promise:

"Verified pharmacies, transparent prices, insurance-aware access, and safer delivery."
