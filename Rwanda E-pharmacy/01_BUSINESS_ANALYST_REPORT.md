# Business Analyst Report

## Executive Summary

Rwanda E-Pharmacy should be built as a two-sided and institutional platform:

- Patient side: search, prescription upload, reservation, insurance support, delivery.
- Pharmacy side: stock management, demand generation, reservations, analytics.
- Institutional side: insurance coverage intelligence, shortage monitoring, aggregated medication data.

The highest-value wedge is not delivery. It is **verified medicine availability**. Delivery becomes a paid service after trust and inventory accuracy are established.

## Customer Segments

### Primary Customers

- Patients and caregivers searching for medicine.
- Chronic disease patients needing regular refills.
- Pharmacies needing inventory and demand tools.

### Secondary Customers

- Insurance providers.
- Hospitals and clinics.
- Delivery partners.
- Health-adjacent advertisers.

### Institutional Beneficiaries

- Ministry of Health.
- Rwanda FDA.
- RSSB and other insurers.
- Research institutions.
- District health teams.

## Value Proposition

Patients:

- Save time and transport cost.
- Avoid visiting multiple pharmacies.
- See verified availability and prices.
- Check insurance support.
- Reserve before travelling.

Pharmacies:

- Receive qualified patient demand.
- Improve inventory control.
- Reduce stock-outs.
- Manage reservations.
- Gain analytics on medicine demand.

Insurers:

- Understand medicine demand and coverage pressure.
- See pharmacy participation.
- Reduce confusion around supported medicines.

Government and institutions:

- See aggregated medicine availability and demand.
- Detect stock-out patterns.
- Support policy and procurement planning.

## Market Differentiation

Rwanda E-Pharmacy should differentiate through:

- Verified pharmacy network.
- Stock accuracy and last-updated signals.
- Insurance-aware search.
- Prescription workflow.
- Public health analytics.
- Regulatory-first operating model.

Generic e-commerce competitors can deliver products. Rwanda E-Pharmacy should win by being safer, more trusted, more integrated, and more useful for health institutions.

## Revenue Model

### 1. Pharmacy SaaS

Free basic listing during pilot. Paid tiers later:

- Inventory Pro.
- Multi-branch dashboard.
- Demand forecasting.
- CSV/API integration.
- Advanced reporting.

### 2. Delivery Fees

Fee per eligible delivery. Delivery must respect medicine safety, pharmacist approval, and regulatory rules.

### 3. Institutional Dashboards

Aggregated dashboards for insurers, research institutions, and approved public health partners.

### 4. Ethical Health Advertising

Ads from private clinics, diagnostics, cosmetics, wellness, and approved health services. No unsafe medical claims. No hidden paid ranking for medicines.

### 5. Integration Services

Paid onboarding and integration for pharmacy chains, insurers, hospitals, and distributors.

## Pricing Hypothesis

Pilot:

- Patients: free search.
- Pharmacies: free onboarding for selected partners.
- Delivery: basic service fee.
- Institutions: grant-funded dashboards.

Scale:

- Pharmacy subscription: tiered monthly fee.
- Delivery: per order fee.
- Ads: monthly campaign packages.
- Institutional analytics: annual contract.
- Integrations: setup plus support fee.

## Key Partnerships

- Rwanda FDA for regulatory alignment and pharmacy verification process.
- Ministry of Health for digital health alignment.
- RSSB and private insurers for coverage rules.
- Pharmacies and pharmacy associations.
- Hospitals and clinics for prescription workflow.
- Telecom/SMS providers for notifications.
- Delivery partners for medicine-safe logistics.

## KPIs

Market KPIs:

- Verified pharmacies onboarded.
- Monthly active patients.
- Search-to-found rate.
- Reservation conversion rate.
- Repeat chronic refill users.
- Pharmacy subscription conversion.

Operational KPIs:

- Stock update freshness.
- Reservation response time.
- Delivery success rate.
- Support ticket resolution time.
- Complaint rate.

Impact KPIs:

- Average time saved per patient.
- Failed pharmacy visits avoided.
- Stock-outs detected.
- Price anomalies detected.
- Medicine availability reports generated.
